The Global Pattern of Climate

Back
The global pattern of climate refers to the distribution of different climate types across the Earth’s surface.

The global climate can be broadly classified into several major types based on temperature, precipitation, and other climatic characteristics. Here are some of the main types of global climate:

TROPICAL MOIST CLIMATE

Major types (based on seasonal distribution of rainfall): Tropical wet (Af), tropical monsoon (Am), and tropical Wet and dry (Aw).

1. Tropical Wet Climate (Af): The tropical wet climate is characterized by high temperatures and abundant rainfall throughout the year. It is typically found in equatorial regions.

2. Tropical Monsoon Climate (Am): The tropical monsoon climate is characterized by distinct wet and dry seasons. It is typically found in regions adjacent to the tropical wet climate.

3. Tropical Wet and Dry Climate (Aw): The tropical wet and dry climate is characterized by distinct wet and dry seasons, but with less rainfall overall compared to the tropical wet climate. It is typically found in regions on the fringes of the tropical wet climate.

DRY CLIMATES

Major types: arid (BW) —the “true desert”—and semiarid (BS).

1. Arid Climate (BW): Arid climates are commonly referred to as “true deserts.” They are characterized by extremely low precipitation levels and high evaporation rates, resulting in dry and barren landscapes.

2. Semiarid climates: are transitional regions between arid and more humid climates. They receive slightly more precipitation than arid regions but still have limited water availability.

MOIST SUBTROPICAL MID-LATITUDE CLIMATES

Major types: humid subtropical (Cfa), marine (Cfb), And dry-summer subtropical, or Mediterranean (Cs).

1. Humid Subtropical Climate (Cfa): The humid subtropical climate is characterized by hot and humid summers, mild winters, and a relatively high amount of precipitation throughout the year. It Is typically found in the eastern coasts of continents.

2. Marine Climate (Cfb): The marine climate is characterized by mild, damp winters and cool summers. It is influenced by the proximity of large bodies of water, such as oceans or seas, which moderate the temperature. This climate type is often found in coastal regions.

3. Dry-Summer Subtropical or Mediterranean Climate (Cs): The dry-summer subtropical or Mediterranean climate is characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. It is typically found in regions surrounding the Mediterranean Sea.

MOIST CONTINENTAL CLIMATES

Major types: humid continental with hot summers (Dfa), humid continental with cool summers (Dfb), And subpolar (Dfc).

1. Humid Continental Climate with Hot Summers (Dfa): The humid continental climate with hot summers is characterized by warm to hot summers and cold winters. This climate type is typically found in the interior regions of continents, away from moderating influences of large bodies of water.

2. Humid Continental Climate with Cool Summers (Dfb): The humid continental climate with cool summers is characterized by mild to cool summers and cold winters. This climate type is also found in the interior regions of continents, but with cooler summer temperatures compared to the Dfa climate.

3. Subpolar Climate (Dfc): The subpolar climate is characterized by short, cool summers and long, cold winters. This climate type is found in high-latitude regions near the poles.

POLAR CLIMATES

Major types: polar tundra (ET) and polar ice caps (EF).

1. Polar Tundra Climate (ET): The polar tundra climate is characterized by extremely cold temperatures and a short, cool summer season. It is typically found in high-latitude regions near the Earth’s poles.

2. Polar Ice Caps Climate (EF): The polar ice caps climate is characterized by extremely cold temperatures and permanent ice cover. It is found in the regions near the Earth’s poles.
